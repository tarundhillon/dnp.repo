Planing & Monitoring

Project Management Office (PMO) function introduces a structured approach, implementation discipline and delivery control within a programme and a strong one also introduces best practices across the entire programme. Traditionally delivery control requires regular status reviews to monitor the progress against plans. The reports required to support reviews are static and often driven by manual processes and ad-hoc data. These reports are cumbersome and slow to generate and are generally riddled with manual data errors.

We believe a PMO function needs to be underpinned by accurate and effective data to make smatter and quicker decisions. We specilise in dealing with ad-hoc data to automated and visualise dynamic reports. The examples below demonstrates some of the key features of the dynamic reports generated from ah-hoc data.
